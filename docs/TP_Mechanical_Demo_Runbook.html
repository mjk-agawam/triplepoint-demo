Background — Who Is TriplePoint and Why Are We Demoing?

TriplePoint MEP is a PE-backed roll-up of mechanical, electrical, and plumbing (MEP) contractors. They have approximately $534M in combined revenue across 8 operating companies (OpCos). They are headquartered in the Cincinnati, OH area. Their private equity sponsor is driving them to standardize operations across all OpCos to create a scalable platform for future acquisitions.

Terry Vanecek is the Service Director for TP Mechanical (the mechanical/HVAC arm). He manages field technicians across the Cincinnati metro area who service commercial HVAC equipment — primarily chillers, air handlers, and refrigeration systems for large facilities like data centers, hospitals, and commercial buildings. Terry's team operates under PM (preventive maintenance) service agreements with their customers.

Terry's Pain Points (From Discovery Calls)

These are the specific problems Terry described in his own words during discovery. The demo is designed to address each one:

"We've sent unqualified techs to jobs." — Dispatchers don't know who holds EPA 608 certification (required for refrigerant handling on large chillers). They find out when the tech is already on-site and can't complete the work. Wasted truck roll = $800+ per incident.

"Our billing cycle is too slow." — Work gets completed in the field, but the paperwork chain between field → office → estimating → invoicing is disconnected. Scope changes discovered on-site get relayed by phone, then re-entered into a separate quoting system. Revenue leaks through the cracks.

"I can't see technician utilization or margin by technician." — Terry has no real-time dashboard. He manually compiles metrics from spreadsheets at month-end. When he acquires a new OpCo, they bring a completely different spreadsheet. There are no standardized KPIs across the 8 operating companies.

"We're juggling between ServiceMax, spreadsheets, and phone calls." — There is no single system of record. Case intake, dispatch, field notes, and quoting live in separate tools with no integration between them.

Demo Story Arc

The demo follows a single realistic scenario end-to-end: Omega, Inc. (a managed-services customer of TP Mechanical) calls in because their York 500-ton centrifugal chiller is tripping on high discharge pressure at their Cincinnati data center. The server room is at risk. We follow the job from case intake through dispatch, field diagnosis, replacement quote, and up to the executive KPI dashboard — showing how Salesforce Field Service replaces every spreadsheet and phone call in that chain.

Competitive Context — What We're Up Against

TriplePoint is currently evaluating ServiceMax alongside Salesforce. Some of their OpCos already use ServiceMax today. The PE sponsor (and Terry) want to see whether Salesforce can provide a single-platform alternative that covers service, quoting, and analytics — vs. running ServiceMax for field service alongside a separate CRM and quoting tool.

Key differentiation to emphasize: Salesforce provides a single platform (Service Cloud + Field Service + Revenue Cloud + Analytics) where ServiceMax requires bolt-on integrations for CRM, quoting, and analytics. For a roll-up doing acquisitions, the "one platform" story is critical — every new OpCo slots into the same system vs. another integration project.
